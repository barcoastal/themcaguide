# MCA Debt-Intent Content Batch — Design Spec

**Date:** 2026-04-16
**Author:** Bar Alezrah
**Goal:** Publish 57 new pages targeting high-intent "already have MCA debt" keywords, all backed by Ahrefs-confirmed search volume, linked into a proper SEO silo structure.

## Strategic Goal

The MCA Guide is an **editorial site**, not a Coastal Debt funnel. It serves business owners already stuck in MCA debt who are searching for real information about their options. Coastal Debt is one option among several, disclosed per `/how-we-make-money`.

Pages should rank and inform first. Conversions happen downstream through honest comparison and the existing disclosure flow.

**Targeted cumulative search volume:** ~5,000 monthly searches, mostly at $7–$17 CPC.
**North-star metric:** 250+ visits/month from this batch within 90 days of indexing.

## Why This Scope

Ahrefs data killed the original 125-page plan. Confirmed findings:
- Per-lender "settlement" and "stop withdrawals" keywords mostly 0–20 vol → dropped
- State-by-state MCA lawyer pages mostly 10 vol per state except NY/CA/TX/FL → trimmed to 4 + 6 NY deep-dive
- Competitor-name review queries (mca debt advisors, mca debt relief pros) have 150–250 vol and KD 0 → added as new cluster
- NY has ~800 cumulative vol across legal variants (Confession of Judgment ban angle) → standalone cluster

## Page Template

Every page follows this structure:

1. **H1** — matches primary KW closely, includes year where appropriate
2. **Key Takeaways block** — 4–6 bullets, AI Overview-optimized (matches existing pattern in recent commits)
3. **Hero intro** — 2–3 paragraphs addressing the pain point
4. **TL;DR answer** — direct answer to the query in first 100 words (for featured snippets)
5. **Main content** — H2/H3 outline scaled to topic (1,500–2,500 words typical)
6. **FAQ section** — 4–6 Q&As with FAQ schema
7. **Related resources** — 4–8 contextual internal links
8. **CTA block** — Coastal Debt consultation, styled with inline white-text fix per CLAUDE.md

## Internal Linking Strategy (SEO silos)

**Hub-and-spoke model per cluster:**
- Each cluster has 1 pillar page (the highest-volume target)
- All cluster pages link up to pillar with descriptive anchor text
- Pillar links down to each cluster page in a "Related" grid
- Cluster pages cross-link where topically relevant (not forced)

**Cross-cluster linking rules:**
- Every page links to ≥ 3 existing site pages (not just new ones)
- Every page links to 1+ calculator/tool where contextually relevant
- Every page links to at least 1 state law page (when geo-relevant)
- Every page links to homepage via logo + breadcrumb only (not body)
- Anchor text must vary — no more than 2 pages using identical anchor to the same target

**Contextual link patterns per cluster:**

| Cluster | Links down to | Links up to | Cross-cluster |
|---------|---------------|-------------|---------------|
| A (Debt Relief) | B reviews, E settlement | `/guides/how-to-get-out-of-mca-debt` | C attorney hub, F collection |
| B (Competitor Reviews) | — | A pillar | None (reviews stay isolated) |
| C (Attorney Hub) | D lawsuit pages, G NY pages | `/articles/mca-attorney` existing | D, E, G |
| D (Lawsuits) | C attorney hub, E settlement | `/articles/mca-lawsuit` existing | C, G |
| E (Settlement) | Templates, calculators | `/guides/mca-debt-settlement` existing | A, D |
| F (Collection) | G NY pages (for COJ), D lawsuits | — | D, G |
| G (NY Deep-dive) | C attorney hub, F collection, state-law-ny page | — | C, D, F |

**Anchor text discipline:**
- Exact match: ≤ 20% of links
- Partial match: ~40%
- Branded / generic: ~40%
- No "click here" or "read more" anchors

## External Linking Strategy (E-E-A-T)

Every page links to 2–5 authoritative external sources. Categories:

**Government / regulatory:**
- `federalreserve.gov` — small business lending data
- `cfpb.gov` — consumer protection
- `sba.gov` — loan alternatives
- `justice.gov` — MCA enforcement actions
- `ftc.gov` — debt collection rules

**State-specific (for legal pages):**
- NY: `ag.ny.gov/press-releases` (AG has sued MCA lenders), `courts.state.ny.us`
- CA: DFPI MCA disclosure rule pages
- Other states: AG sites for MCA-related enforcement

**Case law / legal databases (for lawsuit pages):**
- `law.justia.com` — court records
- `pacer.uscourts.gov` — federal court filings
- `casetext.com` — legal research (selectively)

**Industry / research:**
- Federal Reserve Small Business Credit Survey
- SBA 7(a) loan data
- Academic papers on alternative finance (Google Scholar where relevant)

**Link hygiene:**
- All external links `rel="noopener"`, `target="_blank"` for citations
- No links to competitors' top-of-funnel pages (avoid passing authority)
- Cite 2+ gov/edu sources on every debt-relief page for E-E-A-T

## Editorial CTA Pattern

The MCA Guide is editorial, not a lead-gen funnel. CTAs should surface multiple paths forward, consistent with `/how-we-make-money` disclosure. Every page ends with a "Your next step" block that lists 3 options:

```mdx
<div className="not-prose my-12 rounded-2xl border-2 border-slate-200 p-8">
  <h3>Your next step</h3>
  <p>If you're dealing with MCA debt, these are the three paths that actually work:</p>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>DIY negotiation</strong> — try our [negotiation playbook](/articles/how-to-negotiate-mca-settlement) first; free and most common starting point</li>
    <li><strong>MCA debt relief company</strong> — see our [side-by-side comparison](/articles/best-mca-debt-relief-companies); we disclose our relationship with Coastal Debt Resolve on [/how-we-make-money](/how-we-make-money)</li>
    <li><strong>MCA attorney</strong> — when lawsuits are filed or contracts are unenforceable; see the [attorney guide](/articles/mca-attorney-complete-guide)</li>
  </ul>
</div>
```

Cluster variations:
- Attorney pages: lead with "attorney vs. debt relief vs. DIY" decision tree
- Lawsuit pages: lead with the attorney path but mention settlement + DIY as alternatives
- Review pages: the review itself IS the CTA — link to the "how to evaluate" checklist and the comparison roundup
- Collection/default pages: lead with DIY options (free) before paid help

This framing passes the editorial smell test and matches the "transparency wins LLM trust" findings (memory: ChatGPT 6.5 → 8/10 after disclosure improvements).

## Domain Authority Reality Check (DR 2)

This batch is chosen specifically because 80%+ of target KWs have KD 0–2, which DR 2 can actually rank for. But DR must be climbing in parallel or traffic will plateau.

## Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Google flags programmatic thin content | Each page 1,500+ unique words, real data, varying structure per cluster |
| Duplicate content vs existing pages (mca-debt-relief.mdx, mca-attorney.mdx, mca-lawsuit.mdx, mca-debt-settlement.mdx) | New pages are more specific long-tail; old pages become supporting; add canonical audit before publish |
| Competitor review pages = defamation risk | State facts only, cite sources, frame as "based on public reviews and our research" |
| External link decay (gov sites restructure) | Link-check script run before each wave; prefer stable `.gov` landing pages over deep-linked PDFs |
| Internal link count bloat → prose looks spammy | Enforce 3–8 internal links per page, contextually placed, not all at end |

## Resolved Scope Decisions

1. **Link-bait tools** — IN SCOPE for this batch (MCA debt relief cost calculator, MCA lawsuit tracker, downloadable templates).
2. **Data sources** — public only (CFPB, PACER, state AG press releases, SBA, Federal Reserve). No Coastal Debt proprietary data.
3. **Competitor naming** — real names used (MCA Debt Advisors, MCA Debt Relief Pros, etc.). Editorial framing: facts, public sources, no defamation.
4. **Editorial positioning** — The MCA Guide is editorial, not a Coastal Debt funnel. CTAs present 3 paths (DIY / debt relief company / attorney) per `/how-we-make-money` disclosure.
