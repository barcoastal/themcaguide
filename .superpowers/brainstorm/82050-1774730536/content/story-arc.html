Enhanced Slideshow (extend existing component)

Use the current Slideshow component but add new features: a bank balance tracker that updates each slide, a timeline bar showing where we are in the story (Month 1, Month 3, etc.), and color-coded highlight boxes for the financials. Same card-based UI, but richer data per slide.

Pros

Cons

B

Story Component (new dedicated component)

A brand new McaStory component designed specifically for narrative + numbers. Each slide has: a chapter title, narrative text on the left, a financial dashboard on the right showing bank balance, daily payment, total owed. The dashboard updates with each slide, numbers animate in red/green.

Pros

  • Purpose-built for the story format
  • Financial dashboard makes numbers visceral
  • More engaging, magazine-style layout

Cons

  • More code to build
  • Only used once (for now)

The Story Arc (both approaches use this)

Maria's Story - 11 Slides
1
Meet Maria - Owns a family restaurant in Miami, 12 years. Revenue: $38K/month. Equipment is aging, needs a new walk-in cooler + kitchen renovation.
Bank: $47,200
2
The Search - Bank says no (credit score 620, no collateral). SBA loan: 8 weeks minimum. She Googles "fast business funding" and gets 6 calls within an hour.
Bank: $47,200
3
The Offer - $75,000 advance. Factor rate 1.35. Holdback 15% of daily revenue. Total payback: $101,250. She signs. Money hits in 2 days.
Bank: $122,200
4
Month 1 - Feels Good - New cooler installed, renovation underway. $40K spent on upgrades. Daily ACH pulls $190/day (15% of ~$1,267 daily revenue). She barely notices.
Bank: $76,300
5
Month 3 - The Squeeze - Summer slows down. Revenue drops to $28K/month. Daily pulls are now $140 but she's also paying staff, rent ($6,500), food costs. Cash gets tight.
Bank: $22,400
6
Month 4 - The Call - Another MCA company calls: "We can get you $50K to help with cash flow." She's stressed. She takes it. Factor rate 1.40. Now two MCAs pulling daily.
Bank: $58,900
7
Month 5 - Stacking Hits - Two daily pulls totaling $330/day. That's $6,600/month just in MCA payments. Rent is $6,500. Food costs $11K. Payroll $14K. Revenue: $32K. The math doesn't work.
Bank: $8,100
8
Month 6 - Overdraft - Bank account dips below zero twice. ACH pulls bounce. MCA company #1 calls threatening legal action. She's not sleeping.
Bank: -$1,200
9
Month 7 - The Reckoning - She adds it up. Borrowed $125K total. Owes $171,250 total. Has paid back $46,200 so far. Still owes $125,050. Her 12-year-old restaurant is at risk.
Bank: $3,400
10
Finding Help - Maria contacts an MCA debt settlement firm. They negotiate both advances down to 65 cents on the dollar. She enters a structured repayment plan.
Bank: $3,400
11
The Lesson - Total cost of the "quick fix": $171,250 for $125K in funding. She could have waited 8 weeks for an SBA loan at $131,000 total. The $40,250 difference nearly cost her everything.
