OnDeck and Rapid Finance both serve small business borrowers who need capital faster than a bank can provide. But they operate very differently: OnDeck has built its reputation on transparency and predictable cost, while Rapid Finance is known for speed (and criticism for unclear fees). This comparison covers the practical differences so you can pick the right one.

<h2 id="quick-verdict">Quick Verdict</h2>

**Choose OnDeck.** In almost every situation, OnDeck is the better choice over Rapid Finance. OnDeck's transparency, consistent factor rates, and business credit reporting give it a meaningful edge. Rapid Finance is only worth considering if you need funding faster than OnDeck can deliver AND your credit or revenue disqualifies you from OnDeck.

<h2 id="side-by-side">Side-by-Side Comparison</h2>

| | OnDeck | Rapid Finance |
|---|---|---|
| Product type | Term loan / short-term financing | MCA, term loan, LOC |
| Funding range | $5,000 to $250,000 | $5,000 to $500,000 |
| Cost structure | Factor rate 1.10 to 1.35 | Factor rate 1.22 to 1.52 |
| Minimum credit score | 625 | 500 |
| Minimum annual revenue | $100,000 | $120,000 |
| Time in business | 1 year | 3 months |
| Funding speed | 1 to 2 business days | Same day to 1 day |
| Transparency | High (clear dollar cost disclosure) | Mixed (unclear fee complaints) |

<h2 id="cost-comparison">Cost Comparison</h2>

OnDeck typically quotes 1.20 to 1.30 for qualified borrowers. Rapid Finance typically lands at 1.30 to 1.45 for the same credit profile.

On a $50,000 advance:
- **OnDeck at 1.25:** $62,500 total repayment
- **Rapid Finance at 1.38:** $69,000 total repayment

That is a $6,500 difference for the same money. Unless Rapid Finance's speed or lower qualification threshold is genuinely necessary, OnDeck saves you thousands.

<h2 id="transparency">Transparency and Hidden Fees</h2>

This is where the two diverge most significantly.

OnDeck provides a clear dollar-for-dollar breakdown of total repayment costs before you sign. The factor rate, total repayment, daily payment, and any origination fee are all disclosed upfront.

Rapid Finance has drawn consistent borrower complaints about unclear fees, final rates higher than initial estimates, and aggressive sales tactics. Multiple reviews mention that the actual cost was higher than what was communicated verbally during the sales process.

**Winner: OnDeck**, clearly.

<h2 id="funding-speed">Funding Speed</h2>

Rapid Finance is genuinely fast. Same-day funding is common for qualified applicants.

OnDeck funds within 1 to 2 business days for most applicants, same-day for repeat customers.

**Winner: Rapid Finance** for pure speed. But the speed advantage is often 1 business day, which is rarely worth thousands in additional cost.

<h2 id="qualification-requirements">Qualification Requirements</h2>

Rapid Finance accepts credit scores as low as 500 and businesses as new as 3 months old. This is unusually flexible.

OnDeck requires 625 credit and 1 year in business.

If your credit score is in the 500 to 624 range, Rapid Finance may be one of your only options. In that case, the higher cost is a function of risk-based pricing rather than opportunistic markup.

**Winner: Rapid Finance** for lower-qualification borrowers. **OnDeck** for qualified borrowers.

<h2 id="best-for-your-situation">Best for Your Situation</h2>

**OnDeck is better for:**

- Qualified borrowers (625+ credit, $100K+ revenue, 1+ year)
- Predictable, transparent cost
- Building business credit

**Rapid Finance is only better if:**

- You have credit score between 500 and 624 (OnDeck will decline)
- Your business is 3-11 months old (OnDeck requires 1 year)
- You absolutely must fund within 24 hours
