NSFs (non-sufficient funds) and overdrafts on your business bank statements are the fastest way to get declined for an MCA. Underwriters scrutinize 3-6 months of bank statements and count incidents carefully. This guide covers what's survivable, what's an automatic rejection, and which lenders are most flexible in 2026.

<h2 id="what-lenders-actually-look-for">What MCA Lenders See in Your Bank Statements</h2>

MCA underwriters examine:

1. **NSF count** — how many times your account went negative
2. **Negative ending balances** — days your account ended in the negative
3. **Returned ACH payments** — especially payments to other MCAs
4. **Deposit consistency** — consistent monthly inflows vs volatile patterns
5. **Minimum average balance** — low averages suggest tight cash flow
6. **Number of daily transactions** — very low transaction volume suggests the business isn't really operating

<h2 id="nsf-tolerance-by-lender">NSF Tolerance by Lender (Typical)</h2>

| Lender | NSF tolerance (last 90 days) |
|---|---|
| OnDeck | 2-3 NSFs maximum |
| Credibly | 3-5 NSFs |
| Fundbox | 2-3 NSFs |
| CAN Capital | 5-8 NSFs |
| Rapid Finance | Higher tolerance, up to 10+ |
| Fora Financial | 5-8 NSFs |
| Greenbox Capital | 5-8 NSFs |

Exact thresholds vary by lender and other underwriting factors. Strong revenue and recent clean months can offset historical NSFs.

<h2 id="automatic-disqualifiers">Automatic Disqualifiers</h2>

These almost always result in decline:

1. **More than 5 NSFs in the most recent 30 days**
2. **Negative ending balance on more than 3 days in a month**
3. **Bounced ACH payments to another MCA provider** (indicates current default)
4. **Closed or frozen business bank account**
5. **Personal-use bank account with minimal business activity**

<h2 id="how-to-improve-approval-odds">How to Improve Your Approval Odds</h2>

If you can wait 60-90 days before applying:

1. **Open a new business checking account** and keep it clean for 3 months
2. **Avoid NSFs** at all costs during this period — set up overdraft protection if needed
3. **Maintain positive balance cushion** of at least $1,000-$5,000
4. **Separate all personal and business transactions**
5. **Pay any outstanding MCAs current** before applying for a new one

Three months of clean statements can take you from "automatic decline" to "approved at factor 1.30" — saving $5K-$10K on a typical advance.

<h2 id="better-alternatives-if-nsfs-are-recent">Better Alternatives When NSFs Are Recent</h2>

1. **Invoice factoring** — if you're B2B, factoring companies focus on your customer's credit and invoice validity, not your bank statements
2. **Equipment financing** — the equipment serves as collateral, making bank statement quality less important
3. **SBA microloans via CDFIs** — Community Development Financial Institutions often work with credit-challenged borrowers at 8-15% APR
4. **Asset-based lending** — inventory or real estate can secure a loan even with NSF history
