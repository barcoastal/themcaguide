Choosing a merchant cash advance provider is one of the most important financial decisions you can make for your small business. Not all MCA companies operate the same way, and the difference between a reputable funder and a predatory one can mean thousands of dollars. or even the survival of your business.

We reviewed the most well-known MCA providers in the market today. This guide breaks down what each company offers, where they excel, and where they fall short. We also include a comparison table so you can evaluate them side by side and a section on the red flags that should make you walk away from any provider.

<h2 id="how-we-evaluated">How We Evaluated These MCA Companies</h2>

Before we get into individual reviews, here is what we looked at for each provider:

- **Transparency**. Does the company clearly disclose factor rates, total repayment amounts, and all fees upfront?
- **Cost**. What factor rate ranges does the provider offer, and how do they compare to the market average?
- **Speed**. How quickly can you receive funding after approval?
- **Terms**. What holdback percentages, repayment periods, and early payoff options are available?
- **Customer Experience**. What do actual business owners say about working with the company?

We also checked whether each provider has faced regulatory actions, lawsuits, or widespread complaints from borrowers. No provider is perfect, but some are significantly better than others.

<h2 id="ondeck">OnDeck</h2>

OnDeck is one of the longest-running names in online business lending. While they are primarily known for term loans and lines of credit, they also offer short-term financing products that function similarly to MCAs.

<h3 id="ondeck-pros-cons">What We Found</h3>

**Strengths:** OnDeck is relatively transparent about its pricing. They provide a clear breakdown of total repayment costs before you sign, and their online platform makes it easy to see what you will owe. Funding speed is strong. most applicants receive funds within one to two business days after approval. They also report your repayment activity to business credit bureaus, which is a genuine benefit that most MCA providers do not offer.

**Weaknesses:** OnDeck's rates can be higher than traditional bank loans, and their minimum revenue requirements ($100,000 per year) may exclude newer businesses. Some borrowers have reported that renewal offers come with higher factor rates than their original advance.

- **Factor Rates:** 1.10 to 1.35 for short-term products
- **Funding Speed:** 1 to 2 business days
- **Minimum Revenue:** $100,000 annual
- **Transparency Score:** High

<h2 id="kabbage-amex">Kabbage (American Express Business Blueprint)</h2>

Kabbage was acquired by American Express in 2020 and relaunched as [American Express Business Blueprint](https://www.americanexpress.com/en-us/business/blueprint/). The platform now offers lines of credit rather than traditional MCAs, but the product still serves many of the same use cases. fast access to working capital with flexible repayment.

<h3 id="kabbage-pros-cons">What We Found</h3>

**Strengths:** The American Express backing adds a layer of credibility and regulatory oversight that standalone MCA companies lack. Their application process is fully online, and you can draw funds as needed rather than taking a lump sum. Monthly fees are disclosed upfront, and repayment terms of 6, 12, or 18 months give you more flexibility than most MCAs.

**Weaknesses:** Because Kabbage has transitioned to a line-of-credit model, it may not be the right fit if you need a large lump sum immediately. Credit limits start relatively low for new customers, and some users have reported that their credit line decreased after the first few months without clear explanation.

- **Fee Structure:** Monthly fee model (2% to 9% depending on term)
- **Funding Speed:** Same day to 1 business day
- **Minimum Revenue:** $3,000 monthly
- **Transparency Score:** High

<h2 id="fundbox">Fundbox</h2>

Fundbox offers lines of credit and invoice financing aimed at small businesses. Their product is not technically an MCA, but many business owners consider it alongside MCA options because of the speed and accessibility.

<h3 id="fundbox-pros-cons">What We Found</h3>

**Strengths:** Fundbox has one of the simplest application processes in the industry. You connect your accounting software or bank account, and they can provide a decision within minutes. Weekly repayment schedules spread over 12 or 24 weeks give predictability, and there are no prepayment penalties.

**Weaknesses:** Credit limits max out at $150,000, which may not be sufficient for larger businesses. The weekly fee structure, while transparent, can add up to a significant effective APR. Some borrowers also note that customer support can be slow to respond during high-volume periods.

- **Factor Rate Equivalent:** Approximately 1.10 to 1.25 (based on weekly fees)
- **Funding Speed:** Next business day
- **Minimum Revenue:** $30,000 annual
- **Transparency Score:** High

<h2 id="can-capital">CAN Capital</h2>

CAN Capital is one of the original MCA providers, having been in the industry since 1998. They offer both merchant cash advances and small business loans.

<h3 id="can-capital-pros-cons">What We Found</h3>

**Strengths:** CAN Capital's long track record means they have refined their underwriting process. They accept businesses with credit scores as low as 500, and their MCA product is available to businesses that process credit card payments. Funding amounts range from $2,500 to $250,000, giving them a wide range.

**Weaknesses:** CAN Capital has faced some regulatory scrutiny in the past, including a pause in lending operations in 2015 and 2016. While they have since resumed and restructured, this history is worth noting. Their factor rates tend to fall on the higher end of the spectrum, and some borrowers have reported aggressive collection practices.

- **Factor Rates:** 1.15 to 1.45
- **Funding Speed:** 2 to 5 business days
- **Minimum Revenue:** $8,500 monthly
- **Transparency Score:** Medium

<h2 id="rapid-finance">Rapid Finance</h2>

Rapid Finance offers MCAs, term loans, and lines of credit. They position themselves as a provider for businesses that need capital fast and may not qualify through traditional channels.

<h3 id="rapid-finance-pros-cons">What We Found</h3>

**Strengths:** True to their name, Rapid Finance can fund advances quickly. sometimes within 24 hours. They offer a wide range of funding amounts (up to $500,000 for MCAs), and their account managers provide a more personal touch than fully automated platforms.

**Weaknesses:** Transparency is a concern with Rapid Finance. Several reviewers have noted that the full cost of the advance was not clearly communicated during the sales process, and additional fees appeared in the contract that were not discussed upfront. Their factor rates can be higher than competitors, especially for first-time borrowers.

- **Factor Rates:** 1.18 to 1.48
- **Funding Speed:** 1 to 3 business days
- **Minimum Revenue:** $10,000 monthly
- **Transparency Score:** Medium-Low

<h2 id="credibly">Credibly</h2>

Credibly (formerly RetailCapital) offers merchant cash advances, working capital loans, and business lines of credit. They have funded over $2 billion to small businesses across the United States.

<h3 id="credibly-pros-cons">What We Found</h3>

**Strengths:** Credibly offers a solid range of products, allowing you to choose the structure that best fits your cash flow. Their MCA product uses a percentage-of-sales holdback rather than fixed daily ACH withdrawals, which means your payments flex with your revenue. Minimum requirements are reasonable, and their application process is straightforward.

**Weaknesses:** Some borrowers have reported that the factor rates quoted initially increased by the time contracts were finalized. Customer reviews are mixed. some praise the speed and flexibility, while others cite communication issues and unexpected fees.

- **Factor Rates:** 1.11 to 1.45
- **Funding Speed:** 1 to 2 business days
- **Minimum Revenue:** $15,000 monthly
- **Transparency Score:** Medium

<h2 id="red-flags">Red Flags to Watch for With Any MCA Company</h2>

Regardless of which provider you consider, there are warning signs that should make you pause. or walk away entirely.

<CalloutBox type="warning" title="Avoid Any MCA Company That...">
Refuses to provide the total repayment amount in writing before you sign. Every reputable provider should give you a clear dollar figure for what you will owe in total. If they dodge this question, they are hiding something.
</CalloutBox>

<h3 id="pressure-tactics">High-Pressure Sales Tactics</h3>

If a provider is pushing you to sign today, telling you the offer expires in hours, or discouraging you from reading the full contract, that is a major red flag. Legitimate funders want you to understand the terms because informed borrowers are less likely to default.

<h3 id="no-written-terms">No Written Terms Before Signing</h3>

You should receive a complete contract with all terms, fees, and conditions in writing before you commit. If a company only provides verbal quotes or vague summaries, move on.

<h3 id="confession-of-judgment">Confession of Judgment Clauses</h3>

A confession of judgment (COJ) is a clause that allows the provider to obtain a legal judgment against you without going to court. Several states have banned or restricted these clauses, but some providers still include them. The [Federal Trade Commission](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/business-guide-ftcs-endorsement-guides) has flagged deceptive practices in small business financing, and COJ clauses are among the most controversial.

<h3 id="stacking-encouragement">Encouragement to Stack Advances</h3>

If a provider actively encourages you to take a second or third advance on top of an existing one, be very careful. Stacking multiple MCAs dramatically increases your daily payment obligations and can quickly become unsustainable. Responsible providers will evaluate your existing obligations before extending new funding.

<h3 id="unusually-high-rates">Unusually High Factor Rates</h3>

Factor rates above 1.50 should be a hard stop for most businesses. At that level, you are paying 50 cents or more for every dollar you receive. Unless you have an extraordinarily high-margin opportunity that requires immediate capital, these rates are rarely worth it.

<h2 id="how-to-choose">How to Choose the Right MCA Company for Your Business</h2>

Here is a practical approach to selecting a provider:

1. **Get quotes from at least three companies.** This gives you leverage and a basis for comparison. Never accept the first offer without shopping around.
2. **Calculate the total cost, not just the factor rate.** Ask each provider for the total repayment amount, including all fees. Use this number. not the factor rate alone. to compare offers.
3. **Read the full contract.** Pay special attention to holdback percentages, payment frequency, early payoff terms, and any penalty clauses. If you do not understand something, ask. If they will not explain, leave.
4. **Check reviews and complaints.** Look at the [Better Business Bureau](https://www.bbb.org/) profiles, Trustpilot reviews, and industry forums. Patterns of complaints about hidden fees or aggressive collection are serious warnings.
5. **Ask about flexibility.** What happens if your revenue drops? Can the holdback percentage adjust? What are the consequences of a missed payment? A good provider will have clear, reasonable answers.

<CalloutBox type="info" title="Pro Tip">
Before you commit to any MCA, check whether you qualify for an SBA microloan, a business line of credit, or invoice factoring. These alternatives almost always cost less than a merchant cash advance. The [SBA Lender Match](https://www.sba.gov/funding-programs/loans/lender-match) tool can connect you with lenders in minutes.
</CalloutBox>

<h2 id="bottom-line">The Bottom Line</h2>

Not all MCA companies are created equal. OnDeck, Kabbage (American Express), and Fundbox stand out for their transparency and reasonable terms. CAN Capital and Credibly offer wider accessibility but come with higher costs and some transparency concerns. Rapid Finance can deliver speed but has drawn criticism for unclear fee structures.

The best MCA company for you depends on your specific situation. your revenue, credit profile, how much you need, and how quickly you need it. But regardless of which provider you choose, always get the total cost in writing, read every line of the contract, and never sign under pressure.

Your business deserves a funding partner that treats you fairly. If a company cannot meet that basic standard, there are others that will.
